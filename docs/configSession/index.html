Repository Procedures

As of 05/13/2016

Our architecture repository is an important to keep step with each other's work. Therefore, this process is critical to ensure coordination between our team and ensure the right and left hand knows what's going on. Each of you must master this process to the point of habit to ensure we don't walk (over-write) each other's work. From my experience, large teams can successfully employ this approach.

  1. Start Rational Software Architect (RSA)
  2. Open All Sub-Fragments



  3. Select COAEA [trunk] > SVN > Update to HEAD



    All package modules should load and indicate proper status with an orange indication in lower right corner of each package. If you do not receive this proper indication, stop work and contact your configuration manager.

  4. Once fragments load properly, send notification to the following enterprise architects indicating your work package (fragment). You do not need to send indication when working in your own personal package. You can copy and paste the eMail column in the table directly into Outlook's To... field.

  5. Perform work activity in select fragment. Once saved, you'll receive a black asterisk, which indicates modifications made to your package workspace.
  6. When you are ready to commit your work activity to the Subversion repository, make sure to save your model work and perform the following steps.
  7. Perform Step 3 to ensure you have the latest model updates.
  8. Select your package (fragment), which should have a black asterisk indicating changes made to the package and Commit to the Subversion repository - SVN > Commit...



  9. When you close RSA, watch for notice to save changes. You may have committed your fragment with required additional saves. If this occurs, perform Step 8 again after saving your work.

I can't express the importance of understanding and following this procedure. Improperly followed, we can lose much time, work and money. I recognize the complexity of the process and there's a measure of risk as a result. However, once mastered, we join an elite crowd of professional architects that collaborate to achieve herculean feats of accomplishment. We should recognize the real benefit - individually we're smart, but together, we're genius...
