ESB Strategy

ESB Task Force,

Enterprise Service Bus (ESB) made its way through the CIO Council (CIOC), Department Directors Advisory Council (DDAC), IT Steering Committee (ITSC) and City Council approval. Although we didn't obtain the budget we requested, I believe there's enough resources for us to implement a Service Oriented Architecture (SOA) strategy and deploy a successful SOA capability. The diagram on the right provides the strategic viewpoint that we should discuss. This diagram and future ones like it represent an essential element to develop an ESB Sequencing Plan. A Sequencing Plan is a document that defines the strategy for changing the enterprise from the current baseline (point-to-point interfaces) to the target service-oriented architecture. It schedules multiple, concurrent, interdependent activities, and incremental builds that will evolve our enterprise. The Sequencing Plan is based on the enterprise architecture and is a living document that continuously matures over time.

This decision represents an exciting new episode in the City of Austin's IT story. Let's meet and discuss our milestone model as we prepare for a vendor statement of work (SOW) to obtain ESB staff augmentation and deploy ESB tooling.
