CTM Capability Area Architecture

  • Enterprise Application and Business Transformation Services
    Provides enterprise technology solutions to support the City and citizen’s needs for information and transparency.
  • Enterprise Infrastructure Management
    Provides multiple departmental customer groups shared infrastructure services including internet and network access, mobility, end point support, communications, security and infrastructure software management that empowers customers to fully utilize their computing resources regardless of location. Deliver consulting leadership on standards, risk analysis, technology enabled policy development, total cost of ownership, facilitating customer procurement choices, and incident response in support of these services. Manage the central Texas Fiber network for six other partner agencies to lower costs and ensure availability. Offer online, self-service and live technology 24/7 support to ensure customers can support their services to citizens.
  • Wireless
    Assists public safety, public service agencies, and other city departments by providing broadband and WiFi network access to include provisioning of voice-recording, radio network, public service and safety vehicles, and flood and early warning system.
  • Support Services Group
    Supports procurement of IT goods and services, develops and manages department budget, and performs IT organizational support.
  • Office of the CISO
    Ensures the adequate protection of information assets and technologies. Identifies, develops, implements, and maintains processes across the enterprise to reduce information and information technology (IT) risks. Responds to incidents, establishes appropriate standards and controls, manages security technologies, and directs the establishment and implementation of policies and procedures. Responsible for information-related compliance.
  • IT Business Continuity
    Ensures critical business functions operate despite serious incidents or disasters, or able to recover to an operational state within a reasonably short period. As such, business continuity includes three key elements to include resilience, recovery and contingency. Risk is a core consideration since business continuity is primarily concerned with those business functions, operations, supplies, systems, relationships etc. critically important to achieve the operational objectives. Performs business impact analysis and risk management for the process of determining the relative importance or criticality of those elements, and in turn drives the priorities, planning, preparations and other business continuity management activities. Develops standards, policies, guidelines, and procedures needed to ensure services without stoppage, irrespective of the adverse circumstances or events.
  • Project Management Office
    Initiates, plans, executes, controls, and closes the work of a team (could include waterfall, hybrid or agile methods) to achieve business specific goals and meet specified success criteria. Coordinates scope and resources to deliver request for council action (RSA), request for proposal (RFP), request for quote (RFQ), request for input (RFI), contracts, budgets, etc. in order to acquire good and services on behalf of business operations.
  • Enterprise Architecture
    Translate business vision and strategy into effective enterprise change by creating, communicating, and improving the key requirements, principles, and models that describe the enterprise's future state and enable its evolution and transformation. This transformation process entails the analysis and design of an enterprise in its current and future states from a strategic, organizational, and technological perspective
