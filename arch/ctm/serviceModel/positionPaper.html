The Value of a Service Model

  • Provides a high-level overview of our services and a useful exercise to take a step back and focus on what the key elements of our business are. Avoid getting bogged down in the details of 'how' things happen and concentrate on 'what' does (or needs to) happen
  • Makes possible to identify key capabilities, for example, the ones that differentiate our business and use this information to ensure focus on the areas of importance in our business, whether this is in defining new projects or ensuring business as usual delivers appropriately
  • Modeled Capabilities in the Conceptual layer and represent what the business does (or needs to do) in order to fulfil its objectives and responsibilities
  • CTM roles and process realize capabilities. The Capability is, therefore, at a higher level than a process and is in the conceptual layer
  • The capability represents the what, whereas the process and people represent the how
