Office of the CISO
Capability Area Architecture

After some dialog with the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), EA pursued an alternative approach to derive the Office of the CISO service definition. Rather than identifying stakeholder value, EA analyzed the Information Security Status Assessment report and extracted relevant goals and objectives. Using these elements, EA took a bottom-up approach to derive the Office of the CISO defined capability. EA tested the draft definition against the report's status assessment and concluded a valid service definition. EA vetted the definition with the CISO. Because service definitions don't typically include reference to stakeholders, this still represents a valid service description.

The model does not define «Objectives» in measurable terms; however, we concluded this as beyond the scope of the current service model work anda good next step activity. Note that the indicated processes are incomplete - there are likely more. However, this approach ensured the capture of assigned responsibility of previously identified Information Technology Service Management (ITSM) processes. Some previously identified ITSM processes were actually identified «Goals» and «Objectives» and indicated accordingly by removal from the model.

Office of the CISO Service Model

CategoryNameDescription
«Capability» Ensure Protection of Information and Technology Ensures the adequate protection of information assets and technologies. Identifies, develops, implements, and maintains processes across the enterprise to reduce information and information technology (IT) risks. Responds to incidents, establishes appropriate standards and controls, manages security technologies, and directs the establishment and implementation of policies and procedures. Responsible for information-related compliance.
«Goal» Establish and maintain security technology roadmap Self evident.
«Goal» Establish and maintain InfoSec council and governance board Self evident.
«Goal» Develop professional security personnel Develops professional security training to ensure with the same job title must accomplish the same training requirements while maintaining a program flexible enough to accommodate individual goals. Prepares for an aging city workforce.
«Goal» Establish and maintain security operations Provides for security operations such as network monitoring, threat intelligence, log aggregation, incident response, etc. Staffed 24 x 7 with relevant disciplines (i.e., firewalls, routers, domain name servers, etc.), works with network operations to share timely information and increase response time.
«Goal» Support public information request processes Standardize public information request responses using digital forensics methods. Employs investigative toolsets, automation and backend connections to email and information systems to ensure consistent responses.
«Goal» Establish security frameworks, policies, and standards Self evident.
«Objective» Standardize security requirements and policies Self evident.
«Objective» Establish application security processes Self evident.
«Objective» Manage network vulnerability and intrusion Self evident.
«Objective» Manage network segmentation and firewalls Separates sensitive systems (e.g., badging systems, security cameras, data repositories, financial systems, etc.) into secure network segments. Maintains network firewalls and triage network outages.
«Objective» Establish and manage end-point security Separates sensitive systems (e.g., badging systems, security cameras, data repositories, financial systems, etc.) into secure network segments. Maintains network firewalls and triage network outages.
«Objective» Establish centralized incident communications channels Identifies, responds, and remedies security incidents. Provides internal and external Web presence to include emergency points of contact, centralized, non-attributional reports on security issues, and standardized operating procedures.
«Objective» Manage physical security architecture Implements standardized badging system, centralized badge issuance, and single source controls (i.e., updates and revocation, etc.).
«Objective» Establish and maintain identity and access management Provides identity management to include user password reset, service desk security questions for password change identity and credential tracking and maintenance.
«Objective» Accommodate personnel security Evaluates current strategies to determine issuance of criminal background investigations (CBI) and physical space accreditation needs such as Criminal Justice Information Systems (CJIS).
«Objective» Manage security awareness training Tailors security awareness training to deliver modules relevant to specific city job roles. Evaluates commercial products and other available options to provide computer-based training (CBT) for city staff. Develops strategy to conduct effective and proper security awareness training.
«Objective» Establish and manage risk register Provides centralized information repository of known security risks. The repository to serves as the source-of-truth for all identified risks, what systems are affected, any proposed strategies, dates and times of notable work that has been performed, and when risks have been remediated, mitigated, accepted, or transferred.
«Objective» Prepare and exercise disaster recovery and business continuity Tests and provides the ability to maintain critical business operations, recover from disasters, and completeness of recovery procedures. Ensures resiliency and disruption of core business functions during adverse disasters and events.
«Objective» Provide secure controls of data Establish data classification policy to ensure proper security controls of non-public information. Includes identity of information owners for compromise reporting.
«Process» Perform Risk Assessment Evaluate process and architecture alignment with best practices, regulatory requirements, and city policy. Create recommendation reports based on findings.
«Process» Audit Security Compliance Based on established scope, perform formal audit of security state. Create written report of findings.
«Process» Perform Digital Forensics and Investigations Based on established scope, perform formal audit of security state. Create written report of findings.
«Process» Respond to Security Incident Provide technologies and processes to support detection, containment, and remediation of security incidents. Maintain compliance with notification and long term remediation strategies as required.
