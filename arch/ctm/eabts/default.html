Capability Area Architecture (CAA)

TypeNameDescription
«Capability» Perform Solutions Analysis Based on business goals and needs, perform a solution analysis to identify optimal IT solutions.
«Capability» Provide Enterprise Solutions Support enterprise business needs using common enterprise solutions and development tools for deploying solutions applicable to the city enterprise. The solution is intended to solve enterprise-wide problems not just individual departmental problems. Enterprise level solution aims to improve the enterprise's productivity and efficiency by providing business logic support functionality. Enterprise solutions are about the display, manipulation, and storage of large amounts of complex data and the support or automation of business processes with that data. Enterprise solutions perform business functions such as order processing, procurement, production scheduling, customer information management, energy management, and accounting. It is typically hosted on servers and provides simultaneous services to a large number of users, typically over a computer network - may include Software as a Service (SaaS) and other cloud solutions. This is in contrast to a single-user application that is executed on a user's personal computer and serves only one user at a time, or a departmental level application serving the needs of just one department.
«Capability» Broker IT Solutions For non-enterprise department needs, provide the expertise to empower departments to manage their own technology. Highly knowledgeable IT staff coordinates proper technology solution (i.e., contracting, security, integration standards, etc.). Provides consulting and advisor service for small to medium size solutions requiring software as a service (SaaS), IT contract management, integration analysis, risk management and standardization when appropriate.
«Capability» Provide Data Services Provides management of redundant, structured and unstructured data, data modeling and analysis, data integration, master data management (MDM) and open public data - includes elements such as data security, data marts and data warehouses, etc.
«Capability» Provide Enterprise Geospatial Information Provide enterprise geospatial solutions and platforms, data creation and management, web services and applications, Geographic Information System (GIS) integration, technical consulting and support, enterprise license management and infrastructure software services.
«Goal» Align to Enterprise Architecture business needs analysis Follow the department business needs analysis and resulting business capabilities, goals, objectives, use case and logical data models identified by Enterprise Architecture (EA). Coordinate with EA any required changes due to financial or technology considerations.
«Goal» Improve customer experience Improve requirements understanding, customer commitment (i.e., leadership support), customer testing support and feedback, well written documentation, knowledge-based articles, customer training, user experience - generally more customer involvement. Communications and joint evolution of strategic plans and technology roadmap. Increase separation between development and operational activity.
«Goal» Keep pace with technology Keep pace with technology such as software upgrades, purchased hardware, emerging technology trends, and staff training. Obtain managed services such as infrastructure support, could be Web development, desktop support, operations and maintenance support, data creations, field data collection, data quality assurance, aerials, planimetric data, training, etc. Procuring and/or outsourcing services, Cloud services, etc.
«Goal» Implement license management License tracking (ex., application portfolio management), compliance, anticipated growth and new business needs.
«Goal» Perform vendor relationship management Facilitate vendor performance, product roadmaps, strategic alignment, department business needs, staffing model, training model, etc.
«Goal» Enable customer self-service Account management, knowledge-base, technical configuration, customer training, mature processes, enterprise services (ex., automated GIS services), guidelines, standards, align strategic plans, etc., to reduced service calls, consolidate redundant efforts, on demand services, increase first call resolution, increased efficiencies, customer satisfaction - to operationalize the management of IT services supported with well written service level agreements (SLA).
«Goal» Empower departments to manage department specific IT needs Provide guidelines, standards, alignment of department strategic plans, and technical configuration, etc. to allow departments to operationalize the management of IT services supported with well written service level agreements (SLA).
«Goal» Perform reference and master data management Data knowledge management, reference (relates to addressing information, directory, telephone, area codes, etc.), become single source for all managed city data. Provide means for a comprehensive understanding and consolidation of common data sources. Extract, transform, and load (ETL) various sources of authoritative data sets into enterprise data warehouses to enable business intelligence, analysis and other data interface requirements.
«Goal» Improve employee experience Awareness of opportunity, leadership training, employee development planning, competency development, flexible scheduling, etc.
«Goal» Provide open public data Exposes relevant city information available to the public using open data standards in a machine readable format to promote transparency, innovation, advanced analytics and economic development.
