Stakeholder Viewpoint

For Enterprise Infrastructure Management (EIM), Enterprise Architecture (EA) took a different approach due to the complex nature of the services offered. The top-down, bottom-up method capitalized on previous Information Technology Service Management (ITSM) work. Through collaboration with EIM leadership, we determined that previous identified ITSM services were actually processes, rather than services (i.e., capabilities). By grouping similar processes (see figure Service with Process), the EIM team identified process types referred as capability. Capability represents people, processes and technology delivering value for a specific purpose. The quality of being capable; to have the capacity or ability to do something, achieve specific outcomes, effects or declared goals and objectives. At the highest level, the Customer Facing Service Model captures the CTM value proposition - i.e., services delivering direct value to the CTM environment (i.e., direct interface with stakeholders).

EIM Service Model

Due to the complex nature of IT infrastructure management, the dependency and composition understanding provides many benefits. First, the architecture provides leadership decision-making from an outcome-focused viewpoint, which can lead to infrastructure management organizational considerations - organizationally, it's important to ensure services are not fragmented under multiple leadership responsibilities. Compositional «Part of» and «Depends On» relationships provide further organizational understanding. Second, process grouping of similar type allows competency determination - the needed skill or ability to do something successfully or efficiently. Finally, the service model allows people in the organization to understand how their work contributes to the overall business outcome - a key principle requested by the IT staff. There are other contributory factors such as goals, objects and relationship to stakeholder outcomes, which lead to improved investment decisions. In essence, a service architecture adds a visible element to share ideas, discuss improvements, reduce misunderstanding and increase overall community communications.

To assist the IT staff in understanding their role with CTM services, EA created the following Service Model with Process (use case). The green circle with blue pentagon indicates a process - the collection of processes exercise the capability (i.e., type of process).

Manage Emergency PowerManages uninterruptible power supply (UPS) hardware and software. Includes management and monitoring software quotes for new AC and DC units, purchase acquisition, data center and telecom room design, delivery and storage of UPS hardware at the warehouse, coordinate deployment, configuration, and installation and testing of UPS units on the COATN/GAATN network. Troubleshoots and resolves any connectivity and installation discrepancies. Works with PC Desktop group to specify and design PC/User systems requirements to support UPS management and monitoring software tools. Actively utilizes UPS software tools to manage and monitor UPS status. Operates a back-up automatic electrical system. Within seconds of a utility outage an automatic transfer switch senses the power loss, commands the generator to start and then transfers the electrical load to the generator. The standby generator begins supplying power to the circuits. After utility power returns, the automatic transfer switch transfers the electrical load back to the utility and signals the standby generator to shut off. It then returns to standby mode where it awaits the next outage. Service includes a power distribution unit (PDU) - a device fitted with multiple outputs designed to distribute electric power, especially to racks of computers and networking equipment located within a data center.
