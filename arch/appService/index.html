Application Service Goals

A capability is people, processes and technology delivering value for a specific purpose. The quality of being capable; to have the capacity or ability to do something, achieve specific outcomes, effects or declared goals and objectives. The city delivers capabilities in the form of services such as provide public safety, manage waste disposal, manage and maintain city infrastructure, and deliver reliable power, etc. See other capability examples in the following diagrams. Sometimes, capabilities depend on other capabilities as shown. Based on these examples, some identified capabilities may later identify as process once we further explore with business. Capability is a container of process - a process is not a capability in itself; rather, process belongs to a capability. Business leaders use capability to identify important goals to improve the select service. Capability area architecture is a model depicting important outcomes (capabilities) in a verb/noun form (i.e., action on an object) and their association (dependency relationship) with other capabilities. The derived models are instrumental in establishing achievable goals and objectives necessary to realize or improve the capability and represents an enterprise strategic viewpoint.
