Process Feedback Submitted to 311
Use Case Model

Scope: When Austin 311 receives Feedback a ServiceRequest311Feedback is created by a Feedback Submitter (Austin 311 Agent or the CSR system). The ServiceRequest311Feedback routes to the 311 Command Center to triage and determine the response required. If the Feedback was internally identified the 311 Command Center coaches the impacted staff. If the Feedback is the responsibility of another workgroup at Austin 311 to resolve the 311 Command Center updates the ServiceRequest311Feedback with their notes and routes the ServiceRequest311Feedback to the appropriate staff for further review. If the Feedback is invalid the process skips the workgroup review and proceeds to resolving the Feedback.

Each workgroup has a Feedback Reviewer responsible for reviewing assigned ServiceRequest311Feedback. If the Feedback was routed incorrectly, the Feedback Reviewer starts the Create Feedback Request and Triage use case to submit Feedback concerning the 311 Command Center's routing, and the Review Feedback use case repeats until the Feedback is assigned appropriately. After reviewing the ServiceRequest311Feedback the Feedback Reviewer determines the response required, and then assigns the ServiceRequest311Feedback to the appropriate staff to resolve.

The Feedback Responder receives the ServiceRequest311Feedback designated for their workgroup's resolution. The Feedback Responder identifies the services and work required to resolve the Feedback and then requests the appropriate services from 311 workgroups, or external workgroups/departments. After requesting services the Feedback Responder updates the ServiceRequest311Feedback with their resolution notes and either closes the ServiceRequest (if no additional response is required), or completes their assigned activity (if additional responses are outstanding).
