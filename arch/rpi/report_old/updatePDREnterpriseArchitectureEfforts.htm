Enterprise Architecture - preparing for the future

Greg,

The enterprise architecture (EA) to analyze business needs for PDR and the residential review process is coming along exceptionally well. Your team (see table below) has done an outstanding job! Greg Hand has been an instrumental resource in pulling together the proper experts to elicit improvements to your business processes. Greg also provided invaluable insight into areas of improvements leading to a successful electronic residential review process. Stacey Wuest’s participation from the PMO has been instrumental as well. I’d also like to extend my gratitude to Don Birkner for his leadership support – without it, this architecture would not have been possible. The architecture is visionary in nature, innovative in execution and fully within the boundaries of an implementable IT infrastructure. The Residential Review Architecture can be seen by following the link. The architecture provides an integrated framework to improve three PDR capabilities to include:

·          Perform Design Review

·          Issue Permits

·          Inspect Construction

So what’s next? We still need to perform a final review with relevant stakeholders to ensure the architecture properly represents business user needs. In addition, Greg Hand and I would like to extend this architecture to include Public Works and Transportation departments (maybe others). I’ve also requested the Chief Technology Office (CTO) to review the architecture to ensure their independent findings are captured in the architecture specifications. Once all these activities are complete, the City of Austin will have a completely vetted, interface focused functional needs architecture to guide the PMO toward your desired future state. With a fully vetted enterprise architecture for the City, EA can assist the PMO in developing a Sequencing Plan to evolve the current baseline to the target architecture.
