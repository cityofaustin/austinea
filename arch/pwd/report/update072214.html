Process Change Order
Use Case

Use Case Scope

Either the Contractor or the Inspector (the Change Order Creator) determine there is a need for a change order. One of them accesses the system and inputs the change order information associated with a CIP Id. The Change Order Creator They both sign the change order and hand off to the Inspection Supervisor for their approval. The System routes to each person in the chain of command for their respective approvals. The System notifies the next person in the chain their time to approve the change order has come, when the previous person approves the change order. The system provides a mechanism do assign a date/time and signature, indicating approval. The System will offer the approval in the following order: Inspector, Inspector Supervisor, Design Professional, Project Manager, Sponsor, Sponsor's Financial Representative, Project Manager Supervisor, COA Sponsors & External Sponsors, and Contract Management.

The system sends notification to the project manager upon Contract Management Division approval. The Project Manager is notified that money is encumbered to FDU's, associated with the change order. The Project manager examines eCapris (COA Financial System) to guarantee the encumbered values match as a form of verification.

Process Change Order Interface Descriptions

Type/InterfaceDescription
Administer Capital Construction Data/
enterChangeOrder		 The Change Order Requestor accesses the system and enters the Need for a Change associated with a project. They specify the Capital Improvement Sub Project Id number, and the system automatically fills in header information. Header information includes: Project name, Department, Funding Codes, Contractor Name, Contractor Address, Contract No., Date, Estimate Number, Vendor Code, Capital improvement project inspector, inspection field office, Capital improvement project inspector, professional engineering field office, project manager, Managing Department, change order no. , page no., and submission date on the system.The Change order requestor then fills in the detail associated with the change: Each Change order has many listed rows of items. Each Item has the following: Item No (alpha numeric from contract specification numbers, Change Order Category: AA (Add Alternate),AB (Acceleration Bonus),AD (Allowance decrease), AI (Allowance Increase), AM(Alternate Methods), CC (Changed Conditions ), CI (Code Interpretation), CO (Close Out), CR (Credit To Owner), CS (Consultant/Contractor/Supplier Suggestion), DC (delay Costs), DD (Design Deficiency), DO (Design Other), HM (Hazardous Materials), LD (Liquidated Damages), RC (regulatory Change), UC (Utility Conflicts), UR (User Request). Item funding resources/ sources, description of item, quantity, unit, unit cost, amount (quantity * unit cost), days added (subtotal for amount), (subtotal for days added), , signatures (contractor's rep., consultant or owner rep, Inspection Supervisor, Project Manager, Sponsor Department, Sponsor Department Financial Rep) and titles / dates. The Change Order screen also provides totals for: Amount and Percent of this change order, Amount & Percent of Sum of previous change orders, Amount and Percent of total change orders to date (inclusive of this new one!) The system Indicates if the Total change Amount is over 5%, or 25% (performance indicator for PMD) which are flags for legal issues with the change order. Additionally, the contractor provides percentage on an item by item basis for how much of the item was provided in support of Minority Businesses, broken up by:African American (MB), Hispanic (MH), Native/Asian American (MA), Minority Business Enterprise (MBE), Women Owned Business Enterprise (WBE) , Non-minority Woman Business Enterprise (NON M/WBE) The Contractor supplies:Original Contract Days, Contract Days to date, Total Contract Days to Date (inclusive of this new one). The Change order Screen includes a Disclaimer statement as follows: "In consideration of the change order, agreed to herein as complete, equitable adjustments, and full and final payment for the contractors additional work, the contractor hereby releases the owner of any and all liability under this contract for further equitable adjustments, including additional time for performance, attributable to such facts or circumstances giving rise to the proposal for adjustment."
