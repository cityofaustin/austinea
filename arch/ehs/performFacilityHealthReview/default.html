Evironmental Health Services
Capability Area Architecture

This report provides an update on the use case to Perform Facility Health Review. We made some substantial changes in the way the use case looks; albeit, the content is the same. Instead of embedding the business rules in one sequence diagram, we abstracted the business rules to an activity model referred to as an Interaction Overview. This approach makes the use case model much easier to understand. Each activity on the activity model has its own separate sequence diagram using the same activity name - there are now five smaller sequence diagrams.

We updated the Capability Area Architecture (CAA) based on our discussions and anticipate the CAA to continue to mature over time. As you may have noticed, we've been able to combine capabilities by identifying common processes - this is the nature of the object-oriented analysis and design (OOAD) method we're using and a key reason this reengineering approach is beneficial. You can select any diagram to open it in your browser to view in a more readable form.

Perform Facility Health Review

Scope: The Applicant uses the system to complete an Facility Health Application to obtain a facility health review. This may include a request for Operating Permit, a facility health inspection for general facility assessment, custodial care inspection, or health facility related complaint. Based on application attributes, the system determines applicable fees for processing with the city's approved Electronic Financial Service. Once submitted, the Facility Health Application is added to a Review Queue for quality review by an Application Reviewer. Once approved and a facility inspection is required, the system adds the reviewed Facility Health Application to an Inspection Queue. The Applicant or the Health Inspector can make modifications to the scheduled Inspection Queue and the Health Inspection Supervisor can reassign Health Inspectors. Based on Inspection Queue, the Health Inspector retrieves assigned inspections using a mobile device to retrieve location, contact, and other details of the facility to be inspected and completes an Inspection Report. Following an inspection, the system automatically updates the Inspection Queue and provides Notification to the Applicant on the details of the inspection and its status, i.e., pass or fail. When an Operating Permit is required, the system automatically generates an Operating Permit for issuance to the Applicant.

Type/InterfaceDescription
Accomplish Inspection and Obtain Operating Permit/
submitFacilityHealthApplication		 Through a Web portal, the system guides the Applicant to the proper application(s) to request a facility health review. This may include a request for Operating Permit when a Certificate of Occupancy for new facilities or remodels, a re-occurring request for an Operating Permit, a facility heath inspection for Change of Ownership, a facility health inspection for general facility assessment, custodial care inspection, or health facility related complaint. The system provides form(s) for Health Inspection Application, Operating Permit Application or both when needed, custodial care inspection, a health related complaint requiring a Health Inspector assessment and Food Handler Employee List. When supporting documentation is required such as Certificate of Occupancy or Change of Ownership, the system provides a method for the Applicant to upload the documentation included with the Facility Review Application. If the Applicant obtained a Certificate to Occupancy through the Planning and Development Review (PDR) use case Obtain Final Inspection, the Applicant enters the PDR reference code instead. The system provides the Applicant the ability to expedite inspections and permits. By doing so, the requested Facility Health Application is given higher priority in the selected queue - i.e., Review Queue and/or Inspection Queue. For Food Handler Employee List, the system correlates / adjudicates known food handlers already existing in the system through the food handler registration process.
Geographic Information System/
retrieveGeographicInformation		 The system retrieves geographic information based on address provided on the Facility Health Application to determine geographic boundaries used in Health Inspector assignment or jurisdiction determination. If the jurisdiction is outside city area of responsibility, the Applicant is provided indication the Facility Health Application is outside city jurisdiction.
Accomplish Inspection and Obtain Operating Permit/
determineApplicableFee		 The system determines the required fee based on fee tables or similar means of fee calculation. The fee table or related lookup must be modifiable by approved city staff. The fee table takes into consideration certain attribute values of the Inspection Application such as recent issuance of Certificate of Occupancy, requests for inspection only application or Change of Ownership requiring an inspection. The system checks the Operating Permit application to determine if there's a change in ownership and the system requires an inspection and notifies applicant of the additional inspection application and fees. Fees may include such items as late fees, compliance verification, fess for previously approved but incomplete submission (such as non-compliance of food handlers), expedite inspections and permits, custodial care inspection etc.

Sequence Diagram: 3. Quality Review Application

Type/InterfaceDescription
Accomplish Inspection and Obtain Operating Permit/
updateReviewQueue
 Once Acknowledgment is received from the Electronic Financial System (if fee is required), the Facility Health Application is added to the Review Queue in date/time order for a quality review by Application Reviewer. Expedited Facility Health Applications are given priority in the Review Queue.
Accomplish Inspection and Obtain Operating Permit/
reviewFacilityHealthApplication		 The Application Reviewer selects items in the Quality Review Queue based on the date/time order and reviews the Inspection Application for completeness and accuracy. Once selected for review, the system removes the selected quality item from the queue to prevent multiple reviews from the same item by other Application Reviewers. The Applicant Reviewer can make minor corrections as need for proper processing. The Application Reviewer reviews the Inspection Request Application and determines the condition of the Inspection Application as either approved or denied. If denied, the Application Reviewer indicates the reason for denial and provides relevant information for the Applicant to correct and resubmit. On resubmission, the system bypasses fees already paid. The system provides a template of modifiable, predefined responses selectable by the Application Reviewer. Each template is customizable based on the profile of the Application Reviewer. The template is used for routine and common errors made by Applicant submissions. If a cross reference code was provided for Certificate of Occupancy, the system automatically retrieves the certificate from EDIMS for Application Reviewer verification. The system allows conditional approval when Food Handler Employee List is incomplete, requiring resubmission by the Applicant; however, the Operating Permit may still be issued. The conditional approval is tracked by the system to ensure follow up completion by Applicant.
EDIMS/
retrieveDocument		 When the Applicant provides a cross-reference code from PDR processes to obtain a Certificate of Occupancy, the system automatically recalls the relevant Certificate from the enterprise document imaging and management system (EDIMS).
Applicant/
reviewNotification		 The system provides an electronic means of communicating with the Applicant throughout the Facility Health Application process. The means of communications - i.e., text message, email, etc. is established on the Facility Health Application. During the initial application process, the text message or email is validated with the system to ensure established and valid source. The Applicant receives a Notification on the status of their Facility Health Application as approved or denied with reason for denial and action required to resubmit. This message also provides indication of Health Inspector assigned, their contact information and the initially planned inspection time and date to include continual updates as the Inspection Queue items are completed, feedback on inspection results and link to Operating Permit if applicable. The system identifies renewal dates/time indications and automatically notifies the Applicant to resubmit to renew Operating Permit when time period nears expiration.
Accomplish Inspection and Obtain Operating Permit/
updateInspectionQueue		 Once the Facility Health Application is approved and a facility inspection is requested or required, the system adds certain application attributes needed by the Health Inspector to the Inspection Queue. Inspection Queue items are stored in date/time order to ensure fair inspection order and expedited inspection requests are given priority in the Inspection Queue. The system automatically updates the Inspection Queue by assigning Inspectors based on area of responsibility or jurisdiction - this is determined by the Geographic Information retrieved during Facility Health Application submission. The Inspection Queue includes inspection items relevant to Health Inspector identified on the by PDR use case Obtain Final Inspection for new facility or remodeled facility. The Inspection Queue manages re-occurring inspection needs such as initial 30 day inspection for new or remodeled facilities and other routine re-occurring inspections requiring Health Inspector.

[Note: The follow up inspection automatically generates an updated Operating Permit based 12 months of conditional permit.]
