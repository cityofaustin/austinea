Enterprise Architecture - preparing for the future

By definition, IT guiding principles are fundamental policy statements about the "role" of IT and the use of "technology" in support of the business. EA provides an architecture "role" and a modeling "technology" that directly supports the planning of IT strategic business direction and EA provides a service role (architect) to guide IT toward the business unit's goals. EA provides strategic direction, helps steer a course of action and provides core themes for IT. Until EA becomes part of our culture, our way of doing business, the center-piece of strategic alignment, and the key integration viewpoint to steer IT direction, it is nothing more than a set of "blueprints" that no one follows. Because IT guiding principles promotes consistency in the IT decision-making process, the EA provides opportunity for this consistency. Some may suggest that they don't understand EA blueprints and therefore do not recognize its value. Analogous to this, you may not fully understand all the architectural symbols on the blueprints for your home; however, would you buy a home that was built without blueprints? You trust the symbol interpretation to your architect to ensure the builder follows your architecture plans. The same is true for EA. The enterprise architect's role is to understand the business user needs and create the IT architecture (blueprints) that delivers on these needs. EA's role is to be the liaison between the business user and IT. The enterprise architect is your key business partner just like your home architect is to your builder. With that said, here's what I propose as a new guiding principle:

Employ Enterprise Architecture

History has shown that even the simplest business need and related technologies gets complicated quickly and frequently business needs and priorities are lost in the maze of technology solutions. To resolve this problem, the city employs professional enterprise architects to assist in business IT strategy development in the form of Enterprise Architecture (EA).

A. Rational

·         Interpreting business users needs into well understood IT solutions is a complicated endeavor requiring architectural assistance to thoroughly understand the IT implications and impacts such as economy of scale, reduced cost, efficiency, effectiveness, consistency, flexibility and reuse opportunities. EA provides a repeatable practice to ensure business needs drive IT solutions.

B. Consequences

1.     Business and IT leadership learn and understand the EA process, purpose and value

2.     EA provides a roadmap and training plan to guide the City

3.     Department Directors Advisory Council (DDAC) considers priorities based on EA analysis and recommendations

4.     Architecture analysis provides a business decision-making framework aligned with DDAC Essential Capabilities

5.     When practical, business systems analysis align with the EA practice

6.     EA is the centerpiece for integrated IT strategic planning

7.     As resources allow, the EA is the definitive source for business user requirements for IT solutions developed internally or externally (i.e. functional needs for statement of work)

8.     EA is the architectural blueprint to test IT solutions

9.     As EA demonstrates value, increase staffing to meet business user demand

Because passive voice sentences unnecessarily add words and change the normal doer-action-receiver of action direction, they make the reader work harder to understand the intended meaning. A sentence in active voice flows more smoothly and is easier to understand than the same sentence in passive voice. It's also timeless providing the same meaning in the present as in the future. The primary reason why we should use active voice verses passive voice is that readers often have to guess what you mean. Sometimes, the confusion is minor but other times it can change the entire meaning to the reader. Based on this idea, I made the following recommended changes in [red]. I did not change the intent or meaning of any principle; rather, added clarity in the form of active voice.
