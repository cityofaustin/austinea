Logical Data Notes:

Court Cost Facts

  1. The system will maintain a ledger of all fees, fines and costs.
  2. Cases will have a Collections flag to indicate if there are collections being acted upon the case
  3. Fees can change on January 1st each year, but the effective date drives which version to use. the date of the offense dictates the fees and fines and the effective date on which to draw.
  4. Court costs are always paid first, before fines and fees.
  5. Fee's are paid last.
  6. There is a MAX and a MIN for every type of fine, and the system needs to be able to take this into account and modify the associated fine to the correct amount, should a judge make a mistake.
  7. On a Deferral, fines are removed. Should the defendant not meet the terms of their deferral, the fines are reinstated.
  8. The Court Clerk must be able to override all fee's, court costs and fines.
  9. The system needs to log each and every change to a case's monies, with a timestamp, the user that effected the change, and a description of why the change occurred.
  10. Collections always incur a fee that is a fixed % monies due on a case. This % can change from time to time, so the collection fee % needs to be configurable within the system
  11. Certain Fines or fees are contingent on age of case, therefore the age of a case must also be factor into the fee's and court costs
