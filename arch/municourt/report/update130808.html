The Municipal Court Management Team developed the attached 'draft / preliminary' requirements spreadsheet for the Municipal Court AMANDA upgrade. Please be advised that the requirements analysis is an ongoing process and updates will be provided in the future as they mature. You can download the spreadsheet by selecting the graphical icon. The intent for this file is to provide CSDC a preview or heads-up on the new functionality and other supporting agreements that Municipal Court would like to see in future releases of the Court Management System and the upgrade to AMANDA. It is anticipated that contracting will deliver these requirements to CSDC to ensure Municipal Court compliance with contract agreements.

We based our analysis on the assumption that all current Court Management System capabilities will be provided in the AMANDA upgrade. Therefore, our requirements largely reflect new functionality the current system does not provide. Filters are setup in the spreadsheet to observe various requirements categories such as Must Have, Desired, and Maturity of the requirement. The spreadsheet is organized according the following legend:

Requirements Legend for columns D & E

ColumnAttributeDescription
Criticality Must Have: Considered an essential improvement to the existing capability.
Expected: Although requirement may already exist in the current system, considered an essential function of the Court Management System.
Desired: Requirement provides significant improvement to the current system, yet not essential to Court operations - i.e., alternative processes could achieve similar results.
Action Further Define: Requirement is not fully mature requiring further definition.
Use Case Required: The Court Management Team is using the Unified Modeling Language (UML) to define business processes by examining the Court Management System as a 'black-box' using a customary UML approach. This allows the Court Management Team to think through the desired processes without specifying the specific solution details - leaving the design space open to the vendor. This approach is considered industry best practice and allows for a clear identification of essential interfaces to achieve the desired result of value (ROV) for the UML Use Case. This process was used for Establish Case File Legal Option and Process Civil Parking Case (identified in the Type column).
Mature Requirement: Requirement was reviewed by the Court Management Team and considered a mature requirement and ready for review and implementation by the vendor.
