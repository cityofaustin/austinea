Post Cash Bond
Use Case Model

Scope: The Customer (who is either the Defendant or another individual posting a cash bond on the case) submits a request to post a cash bond. The Clerk receives the request and begins processing a cash bond on the case (usually for an extension). The clerk retrieves the case file using the case number. The Court Clerk enters the bond amount (full cost of the ticket or the amount set on the Judge's order) and uses the current date (by default) before printing the bond form to be given to the Customer for completion. The Customer completes and submits the form to the Clerk for processing. The Clerk accepts the form and uses the information on it to process the cash bond for the case.
 
If Customer is not the Defendant on the case then the Clerk submits the demographics provided by the Customer on the bond form and searches the case parties for a match. If the Customer is a case party the Clerk sets them as the bond poster. If the Customer is not a case party on the case, the Clerk adds the customer as an additional case party on the case and sets them as the bond poster.
 
The Clerk requests payment for the bond from the Customer. The Customer remits the requested payment to the Court Clerk. The Clerk processes the remittance for the cash bond by setting the bond type ('CB'), payment type and transaction type ('BO'). The Clerk scans and attaches the completed bond form to the case.
 
If the Defendant missed an appearance with the prosecutor the Clerk adds an action to the case indicating that with one of the codes: TRAD, CMAD, ENAD
 
The case management system presents the appropriate docket schedule to the Clerk depending upon information submitted in the bond request. The Court Clerk reviews the docket schedule and suggests the first court appearance date available to the Customer. If the Customer prefers a different date on the docket schedule they present that preference to the Clerk. The Clerk generates the Notice To appear document. The clerk gives a copy of the completed bond form, a receipt for the bond payment and the Notice To Appear to the Customer.

Post Cash Bond
Sequence Diagram

Req#Type/Interface - Requirement DescriptionPerformance
001 Court Clerk/processRequestToPostCashBond
The Customer (who is either the Defendant or another individual posting a cash bond on the case) submits a request to post a cash bond to the Court Clerk. The Court Clerk receives the request and begins processing it (usually for an extension).
002 Manage Court Case/retrieveCaseFileByCaseNumber
The case management system retrieves the case file using the provided case number.
003 Court Clerk/printBondForm
The Court Clerk enters the bond amount (full cost of the ticket or the amount set on the Judge's order) and uses the current date (by default) before printing the bond form to be given to the Customer for completion.
004 Customer/completeForm
The Customer completes the form.
005 Court Clerk/processCompletedBondForm
The Court Clerk receives the completed bond form and processes it.
006 Manage Court Case/processCompletedBondForm
The case management system processes the cash bond for the case by attaching the bond form to the case file.
007 Manage Court Case/setDefendantAsBondPoster
If the bond poster is the Defendant: The case management system sets the sets the bond poster on the case as the Defendant.
008 Manage Court Case/searchCaseParties
If the bond poster is NOT the Defendant: The Court Clerk searches the case parties on the case to determine if the Customer is listed. The case management system performs the search.
009 Manage Court Case/addCaseParty
If Customer is not a case party: The Court Clerk adds the Customer (who was unlisted as a case party) to the case. The case management system adds the customer as a case party.
010 Court Clerk/requestBondPayment
The Clerk requests payment for the bond from the Customer.
011 Court Clerk/processBondPayment
The Customer remits the requested payment to the Court Clerk. The Court Clerk processes the bond payment.
012 Manage Court Case/processBondPayment
The bond type, payment type and transaction types are recorded on the case. The bond payment is recorded on the case. The payment is processed for the bond.
013 Manage Court Case/attachDocumentToCase
The case management system takes the document which has been uploaded and attaches it to the case file. The document can be any document when the use case is "Accept Case File document."
If it is a case generated from a CARL citation the documents are:
  1. The two images of the violating vehicle - position in the intersection and zoomed in license plate.
  2. The citation
If a case is being generated from a parking citation, the document is the citation.
If it is a criminal parking case the documents are:
  1. The affidavit
  2. The placard
  3. The scanned image of the customer's driver license
If a cash bond is being posted the document is the bond.
If a scheduled time payment plan is being established or modified the document is the STPP.
If an appeal is being processed the documents are:
  1. Order ofTransfer (2 copies)
  2. Certificate of Transfer
  3. Approval of Trial Judge
  4. Letter to Defendant
  5. Letter to Prosecutor
  6. Transcription file
  7. The appeal document
If a late representation request is being filed the documents are: Attorney's letter of representation
  1. The motion to proceed
  2. Appearance motion
  3. Emergency order or motion
If a customer has completed a Driver Safety Course the document is the completion certificate.
If a probable cause affidavit is being filed, the document is the affidavit
If an appeal is being initiated the documents are:
  1. The motion to proceed document
  2. The appeal bond
If a general or Youth Services complaint is being generated, the document is the complaint.
014 Manage Court Case/addActionToCase
The case management system adds an action, specified by an action code, to the case (referenced by the case number) to create the action record.
  • If a DSC completion paperwork is being submitted the action reflects the sentence completion.
  • If the action records a missed appearance with the prosecutor then the action codes include: TRAD, CMAD or ENAD
  • If the action records a missed trial then the action codes include: JTRI, BTRI
015 Court Clerk/presentDocketSchedule
The case management system presents the appropriate docket schedule to the Clerk depending upon information submitted in the bond request.
016 Manage Court Case/reviewDocketSchedule
The Court Clerk reviews the docket schedule and suggests the first (or next) court appearance date available to the Customer.
017 Court Clerk/processCourtDateRequest
If the Customer prefers a different date on the docket schedule they present that preference to the Clerk.
018 Manage Court Case/setCaseOndocket
The case management system sets the date on the docket calendar when the Defendant must appear in court before the Judge.
  • If the Judge orders an appearance to review the case or the Court Clerk schedules a court event.
  • If the case management system presented the first available date to the Court Clerk and they secured the Defendants consent to that date then the date presented is set on the docket schedule.
  • If the Defendant prefers another date other than the date first presented and that date is available on the docket schedule then that date is set on the docket.
019 Manage Court Case/generateNoticeToAppear
The case management system generates the Notice To appear document.
020 Customer/takeBondDocs
The clerk gives a copy of the completed bond form, a receipt for the bond payment and the Notice To Appear to the Customer. The Customer receives the copy of the form and the receipt.
