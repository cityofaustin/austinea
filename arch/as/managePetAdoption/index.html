Manage Pet Adoption
Use Case Model

Scope: The Citizen uses online resources (websites, social media, shelter visit, etc.) to research and locate animals of their liking. Once an animal is desired, the Citizen contacts Customer Service and indicates interest. Customer Service will check to determine if the Citizen has prior history, and will ask them several questions related to animal handling and adoption. If any concerns are raised with the quality of the Citizen, a Senior Staff member will vet the Citizen to clarify the adoption issues. If the Citizen is qualified to adopt, Customer Service will have them sign a pet agreement, and will check to see if the pet can be released. If the animal has undergone recent surgery, or has a physical injury, the Vet may determine the animal cannot be released until a later date. If there is no hold on releasing the animal, the Veterinary Technician will request general medical information about the animal be released to the Citizen. Customer Service prints the paperwork and has the Citizen sign the medical summary, indicating they understand the current and past medical history of the animal prior to adoption. Customer Service uses the system to annotate the PersonInfo indicating the signature of the documents and directs the Citizen to the Cashier to pay adoption fees. The Cashier receives payment and provides the Citizen a receipt. Customer Service contacts Animal Care to bring the animal to the Citizen. Once delivered, Customer Service, once again uses the system to annotate the delivery of the animal to the Citizen.

Sequence Diagram

Req#Type/Interface - Requirement DescriptionPerformance
001 Provide Animal Shelter and Rescue/provideAvailableAnimals
The Citizen accesses the City of Austin's Pet Adoption website, or sees an available animal on Facebook or other site such as Petango, Petfinder, adopt-a-dog.com, Instagram, etc.
002 Customer Service/setUpForAdoption
The Citizen contacts Customer Service in person and expresses interest in adopting an animal. NOTE: It is desirable to have this be online instead of in person.
003 Senior Customer Care Staff/vetPotentialAdoptee
The Senior Customer Care Staff talks with the Adoptee, reviews all notes within the Adoptee's PersonID record. Concerns are raised if the PersonID record has a "no adopt" flag, or shows citations or numerous owner surrenders.
004 Citizen/clarifyAdoptionIssues
The Citizen responds to questions/concerns raised by the Senior Customer Care Staff.
005 Citizen/signPetCareAgreement
Customer Service provides the Pet Care Agreement for the Citizen to sign. The agreement includes the following fields:
006 Vet/checkIfAnimalCanBeReleased
The Vet uses the system to examine the animal's information. The Vet conveys pertinent information, and will make a decision as to whether the animal may be released immediately.
007 Citizen/informOfAnimalHealth
Customer Service informs the Citizen that the animal is not currently available for adoption and explains the reason. Often a timeframe is provided for when the animal may be adopted in the future; however, the animal is not "reserved" for the Citizen in any way.
008 Veterinary Technician/requestGeneralMedicalInformationToProvideToAdopter
Customer Service asks the Vet Technician about medical needs or history of an animal in preparation for adoption. Customer Service sometimes requests medications be prepared for an animal that is about to be adopted.
009 Customer Service/printAdoptionPaperwork
Customer Service uses the system to generate an adoption packet, which consists of medical history, rabies certificate and behavioral information.
010 Citizen/signMedicalSummary
The Citizen reviews all documentation and signs the medical summary indicating they understand the current medical history of the animal, as well as the current health condition of the animal and any behavioral observations.
011 Provide Animal Rescue and Shelter/addAndUpdatePersonAndAnimalInfo
Customer Service/Foster Coordinator/Other users will access the system and add or update person and animal info. They will include information like owner, owner address, pet name, pet breed, pet sex, pet microchip info, rabies certificate information, as well as other important information. If the animal or person is already in the system, Customer Service uses the system to update the information. In the case of Trappers, add the date they signed the Standards of Conduct and the Standard Operating Procedure. In the event of Volunteer Applicant for Trappers, the Outreach Education Team will access the system and provide information from the TrapperApplication to create a PersonInfo record for the Applicant (including verification date and time for the signing of the Standard of Conduct, and the Standard Operating Procedure), if one does not already exist. If a PersonInfo record exists, the Outreach Education Team will update the information as necessary based on the Applicant's information. For spay/neuter, the user modifies the sex of the animal from M/F (male/female) to S/N (spay/neuter), and records the date of surgery, date processed, vet clinic ID (the system maintains a list of all vet clinics on record within the area). Foster Coordinators/Rescue Coordinators create PersonInfo records for Citizens acting/assisting with foster care, and annotate AnimalInfo Records with information indicating Foster Care Providers who have taken ownership of the animals.
012 Cashier/provideReceiptForPayment
The Citizen pays the current adoption fee (or provides voucher) to the Cashier. The Cashier uses the system to check the person's information to determine if any fees have been waived (or reduced). The Cashier provides a receipt for the monies received. This includes: date, time, amount, type of payment (cash, check or credit), the animal for which the payment is made (the animalID) and the PersonID. NOTE: It is desirable for the POINT OF SALE system to integrate with AIMs to record the money receipt. On reclaim, fees may be waived if the animal is already spayed/neutered and microchipped, or if the owner is expedient in their attempt to reclaim.
013 Animal Care/deliverAnimalToAdopter
Customer Service uses a radio to contact Animal Care and request an animal be brought to the front for adoption. Customer Service provides the Kennel No. where the animal resides, or if multiple animals share a cage, the AnimalId/microchip information.
