Manage Animal Reclaim
Use Case Model

Scope: The Animal Source (a Citizen, Field Officer, or Animal Owner) brings an animal to the City of Austin Animal Shelter, and the standard "Intake Animal" process is followed to check the animal's health, provide vaccines, and check for a microchip. Following intake, the animal's information is sent to the StrayMapper website to display the location at which the animal was found. If the animal's owner is not readily available, and the Animal Source is willing to distribute fliers, Customer Service will create "found fliers" for the Animal Source to distribute. If the animal is found to have a microchip, Customer Service will check with the respective microchip's company roster to determine if the owner can be located. If the Animal Source knows the owner, Customer Service takes the owner information. Once the owner information is determined, Customer Service will contact the owner and request they come claim their animal. If the animal's owner comes to pick up the animal, they are required to provide proof of ownership for the animal, and pay any fees accrued prior to the animal's release. If an animal's owner cannot be located within 72 hours, the animal is made available for general adoption with the public.

Manage Animal Reclaim
Sequence Diagram

Req#Type/Interface - Requirement DescriptionPerformance
001 Customer Service/assistFindingAnimalOwner
The Animal Source arrives with animal and Customer Service takes possession of the animal (if the Animal Source is not willing to keep them).
002 StrayMapper Website/addAnimalToMap
The system checks all animals marked as strays, formats an email and sends the information to StrayMapper. Information included is location, date, time and description of animal.
003 Provide Animal Rescue and Shelter/createFoundFliers
Customer Service uses the system to create fliers with animals information, picture of the animal, animal, ID, where the animal was found, the City of Austin Animal Services address and contact information.
004 Customer Service/distributeFliers
The Animal Source distributes fliers in hopes of locating the animal owner.
005 Provide Animal Rescue and Shelter/addAndUpdatePersonAndAnimalInfo
Customer Service/Foster Coordinator/Other users will access the system and add or update person and animal info. They will include information like owner, owner address, pet name, pet breed, pet sex, pet microchip info, rabies certificate information, as well as other important information. If the animal or person is already in the system, Customer Service uses the system to update the information. In the case of Trappers, add the date they signed the Standards of Conduct and the Standard Operating Procedure. In the event of Volunteer Applicant for Trappers, the Outreach Education Team will access the system and provide information from the TrapperApplication to create a PersonInfo record for the Applicant (including verification date and time for the signing of the Standard of Conduct, and the Standard Operating Procedure), if one does not already exist. If a PersonInfo record exists, the Outreach Education Team will update the information as necessary based on the Applicant's information. For spay/neuter, the user modifies the sex of the animal from M/F (male/female) to S/N (spay/neuter), and records the date of surgery, date processed, vet clinic ID (the system maintains a list of all vet clinics on record within the area). Foster Coordinators/Rescue Coordinators create PersonInfo records for Citizens acting/assisting with foster care, and annotate AnimalInfo Records with information indicating Foster Care Providers who have taken ownership of the animals.
006 Facebook/postMissingAnimalInformation
Customer Service accesses Facebook and posts information about the animal in an attempt to find an owner. The Customer Service personnel will construct an appealing story, post pictures and information about where the animal was found. Whether a Facebook entry is made for a stray is totally subjective based on the workload.
007 Customer Service/researchAnimalOwnerFromMicrochip
Customer Service uses the scan code and searches the system looking for a match. If there isn't one, then Customer Service will contact the microchip company (determined by the first few characters of the code or code length). NOTE: It would be desired to have the system automatically know the microchip vendor based on code and interface with that company. Currently all microchip companies require phone calls. The microchip company will call the person registered and this may be the owner. It may not be if the ownership has been transferred after registration. This may lead to calls to Veterinarians or other animal shelters or animal welfare agencies in an attempt to find the real owner of the animal. If an email is provided, then an email will be sent to the owner.
008 Customer Service/attemptToContactOwner
Customer Service takes the owner's information the Animal Source has and attempts to reach the animal's owner. Customer Service will leave messages including information about how to reclaim the animal. If an email is provided, then an email will be sent to the owner. The owner is given three days to pick up their animal (based on city ordinance).
009 Animal Owner/requestOwnerReclaimAnimal
Customer Service tells the owner they have 72 hours to get their animal, as well as the location at which the animal can be picked up, hours of operation, outstanding fees, and any animal health issues discovered while the animal was at the shelter. Sometimes, in case of medical emergency, the deadline may be sooner than 72 hours.
010 Animal Owner/provideProofOfOwnership
The Animal's Owner must provide proof of ownership. This is achieved by providing pictures, vet records, description of distinguishing marks, area lost matches are found, microchip paperwork, adoption paperwork, etc.
011 Cashier/provideReceiptForPayment
The customer pays the current adoption fee (or provides voucher) to the Cashier. The Cashier uses the system to check the person's information to determine if any fees have been waived (or reduced). The Cashier provides a receipt for the monies received. This includes: date, time, amount, type of payment (cash, check or credit), the animal for which the payment is made (the animalID) and the PersonID. NOTE: It is desirable for the POINT OF SALE system to integrate with AIMs to record the money receipt. On reclaim, fees may be waived if the animal is already spayed/neutered and microchipped, or if the owner is expedient in their attempt to reclaim.
