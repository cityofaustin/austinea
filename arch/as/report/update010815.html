Perform Veterinary Services
Use Case

Use Case Scope

Upon arrive the arriving animal is given a cursory examination by the technician and a decision is made about the animal. It is either in healthy condition, needs attention (but not an emergency), or needs immediate attention. Healthy Animals and non-emergency animals are examined by the Intake vaccinator, who will administer required vaccines and update the animal information in the system. In Emergency situations, the veterinarian is paged via the overhead paging system and the Vet performs an examination. In parallel, the Floating Technician examines the animal, searches for microchip, and updates animal information. Additionally the Floating Technician performs requested diagnostics and administers treatments as dictated by the vet. Then the Vet examines and evaluates the animal's condition for a period of time until they are well enough to be moved to general care. The technician then schedules for an animal to be moved... sometimes to general kennels, sometimes to rabies or worm quarantine areas. The Animal information is once again updated in the system to indicate the location of the animal. If the veterinarian has a scheduled appointment for animal evaluation, they will examine the animal, and a floating technician will perform any diagnostics required. If treatments are needed, the Veterinary will schedule them in the system.

Perform Veterinary Services Interface Descriptions

Type/InterfaceDescription
Technician/
performInitialEvaluation		 The Technician performs an initial cursory examination of the the animal to determine the appropriate care to administer to the animal.
Intake Vaccinator/
examineAndManagePetIntake		 The Intake vaccinator records and confirms breed, age, sex, color, body condition score, weight, tattoos, spay scars, scan for microchip, behavior, look for signs of illness or injury, temperature (if needed), tags, collar, harness.
Intake Vaccinator/
administerVaccine		 The Intake Vaccinator administers the vaccines and dewormers deemed appropriate. Based on intake type and age, additional lab work may be performed.
Provide Animal Shelter and Rescue/
updateAnimalInfo		 The User uses the system to update information on the Animal info record. This includes specifying Memo's, and providing delivery information, and recording any fees incurred with animal delivery and care. Enter all medical, behavior and preventive care administered to the animal.
Provide Animal Shelter and Rescue/
scheduleVetExam		 The user documents the problem or reason for Vet attention, documents their findings, includes the weight and temperature. The System adds the needed evaluation to the vet work Queue.
Provide Animal Shelter and Rescue/
pageVet		 The Technician uses PA or Phone to audibly summon an available Vet currently in the building.
Vet/
performVetExamine		 The Vet performs a physical examination of the animal, considers its history and to determines whihc diagnostics and treatments are needed. If needed, they will perform procedure/Surgery.
Float Technician/
examineAndManagePetIntake		 The Floating Technician records and confirms breed, age, sex, color, body condition score, weight, tattoos, spay scars, scan for microchip, behavior, look for signs of illness or injury, temperature (if needed), tags, collar, harness.
Provide Animal Shelter and Rescue/
scheduleAnimalMove		 The user accesses the system and schedules the animal to be moved. The User specifies, the date and time for the animal relocation. The User indicates whether to quarantine the animal (due to rabies, etc.) or move to public population, non-public, or isolation.
Float Technician/
performDiagnostics		 The Float technician performs laboratory/Diagnostic tests ordered by the veterinarian.
Float Technician/
administerTreatments		 The Float technician administers treatments ordered by the veterinarian.
Vet/
evaluateAnimalCondition		 The Vet rechecks the animal to determine status of treatments and to update the animals condition, diagnosis, etc.
Provide Animal Shelter and Rescue/
scheduleAnimalTreatment		 The Vet Schedules future treatments (drugs, frequency, and amounts, duration, etc).
