Understanding Roles between EA and PMO

The following position paper explains the boundary issues regarding the project management office and enterprise architecture. A more comprehensive white paper is at Understanding Roles between EA and PMO.

Where's the boundary between project management responsibilities and enterprise architecture?

  • Project managers and their staff frequently develop business user needs to include technical and functional requirements
  • Enterprise architects employs a method yielding technical standards and business functional needs

[Note: Clearly, there's a competing role…]

Enterprise Architecture is Innovative

  • Innovation is not invention
  • Innovation is doing something different
  • Innovation is frequently disruptive!

[Note: Not everyone benefits from the change innovation creates.]

Differentiation between PM and EA

  • Project Management initiates, plans, executes, controls, and closes the work of a team to achieve specific goals to meet specific success criteria (objectives)
  • Enterprise Architecture is a process of translating business vision and strategy into business goals and objectives

[Note: EA works with business to create goals and objectives and the PMO executes goals and objectives - this creates the perception that the PMO is working for EA... However, in reality, EA and the PMO both work for business. I suspect this is our problem…]

Roles

  • EA is responsible to assist business leaders in vision, mission, capability, goal and objective identification leading to a comprehensive use case model describing visionary use of IT business systems
  • Architecture includes the people, process and technology to accomplish the business mission while achieving the business target vision through select business goals (not just IT goals!)
  • EA derives sequencing plans from capability area architectures
    • Establishes the relationships (i.e., dependencies) between business capabilities (i.e., services) and business goals
    • Process includes the identity of important goal dependencies and their influence on important business outcomes to accomplish the mission
  • EA doesn't tell business people or the PMO what to do
    • EA facilitates the discussion and provides a process between the PMO and business leadership to create a sequencing plan to ensure achievement of the business vision
  • EA reports to business leaders to create business architectures to guide IT project management to successful business solutions
    • On approval, the business stakeholder authorizes delivery of the business architecture to IT project management
  • IT project management uses the business architecture to plan implementation (proposal development, contracting, coordinate resource needs, planning, outsourcing strategies, etc.)
    • Identified changes to the business architecture is coordinated with enterprise architecture

[Note: Approach is fully compliant with the EA Roadmap…]
