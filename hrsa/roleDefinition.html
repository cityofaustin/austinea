Human Resources Department Role Definitions

Note: All roles were not defined; however, most are self explanitory based on their name and the context for which they are used on the activity models.

NameDefinition
Applicant Completes application, required assessments and testing.
Assistant Director Assistant to the Direct of any given department.
Associate Accountant Accountant in the Human Resources Department (HRD), Finance Division. Provides services such as ordering supplies, providing receipts, and reimbursements, handling petty cash, and managing time sheets, and collecting performance measures.
Benefits Vendor Vendor selected to administer various employee benefit services.
Civil Service Coordinator Oversees activities adhering to chapter 143 of the Texas Local Government Code such as disciplinary hearings, civil service promotional exams, and entrance exams. The departments serviced include Austin Police Department, Austin Fire Department, and Austin Travis County Emergency Medical Services.
Complaint Recipient Supervisor, Manager, Human Resources or Department Director who receives a complaint regarding a personnel policy issue.
Department HR Liaison Department single point of contact (SPOC) to Corporate HRD.
Drug and Alcohol Tester Third party company engaged to perform drug tests on employees.
Employee Benefits Division Consultant Oversees activities of vendors and third party administrators in the development of new or modified benefits programs.
Employee Benefits Division Manager Analyze employee and business needs for benefits programs and oversees benefits activities.
Employee Benefits Division Specialist Provide education to employees and retirees on benefits programs.
Employee Benefits Division Staff Oversees activities of vendors and third party administrators in the development of new or modified benefits programs. Provide education to employees and retirees on benefits programs.
Enrollee Consist of employees, retirees and their dependents covered on benefits programs.
Hiring Facilitator Assist the Hiring Manager in facilitating the selection of screened and qualified candidates in compliance with city policies and guidelines. Based on a city job posting, candidates submit applications. Once application period ends, candidates are screened, preparations for interview accomplished, interview is conducted and top candidate selected following positive background checks. For executive level and high profile positions, HRD plays the role of Hiring Facilitator; otherwise, the Hiring Facilitator is a department role.
Hiring Manager Identifies open positions, competences required, determines parameters for the posting, determines posting period, and posting location (internal/external/Web site). Screens applicants, prepares for interview, identifies top candidate, and makes final offer.
Hiring Quality Assurance Representative Ensures consistency of the posting process.
HR Department Manager Ensures consistency of the posting process.
Interview Panel Understand Hiring Manager needs and wants. There may be several panels. Review job postings, applications and resumes before interviews. Must attend "Hiring Fundamentals" training. Understand do's and don'ts of interviewing. Participate in interviews. Rank and/or score candidates.
Investigating Department Relevant city department assigned to investigate complaint.
Substance Abuse Professional Third party counseling provided to full time employees failing a drug test. The employee can only have one offense in two years. Otherwise termination is the only option. If they only have one offense, this company is engaged to provide alcohol and drug counseling.
Third Party Administrator Third party contracted to assist in the administration of various Human Resources activities.
